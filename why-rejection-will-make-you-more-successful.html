Why rejection will make you more successful

4 April 2018

Photo by Gemma Evans on Unsplash

“If you aren’t getting rejected on a daily basis, then your goals aren’t ambitious enough” – Chris Dixon.

Nobody likes rejection. If we are lucky, we shrug it off, never to be thought of again. More often, it knocks our confidence and stops us putting ourselves out there. We end up accepting the status quo, believing that we won’t get what we want.

But maybe we are viewing rejection incorrectly. I read an interesting perspective on it the other day. To paraphrase the article, “rejection serves as proof that you’re living life to the fullest. Expect to be rejected sometimes. If you never get rejected, you may be living too far inside your comfort zone. You can't be sure you're pushing yourself to your limits until you get turned down now and then."

When was the last time you were turned down for a better job than you have now? A position with more responsibility? One that stretches you and requires you to manage more people? A job that builds your skill set and involves you growing into the person you need to be to have the career that you desire?

Rejection shows that you are raising the bar on what you want for yourself. Good things happen when you do this.

So today, you may be rejected, but tomorrow will be better as a result.
