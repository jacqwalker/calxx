Our best CV tips

Few people enjoy putting together or updating their CV. To help you with the process, this article will give you some of our best CV tips.

  • Ideally your CV should be two pages and no more than two and a half

  • Many hiring managers will review the first half of the front page, so you want to draw them in from the start. Make sure to include all relevant information in this section.

  • Include your contact number, email address and a link to your linkedin profile in the the headers section of your CV. Do not include your age, martial status or address as this information does not need to be shared with the hiring manager or recruiter

  • Your CV needs to detail what you have accomplished. Avoid making it sound like a job description e.g. avoid the words 'I was sresponsible for'

  • Begin your CV with a personal statement - a snapshot of your experience to date e.g. your qualification, number of year PQE, the sectors you have worked in and your key skills

  • Following your personal statement, include a section listing your career achievements

  • List your experience, starting with your most recent experience. Make sure to explain any gaps eg sabbatical, career break

  • Include a summary of what the company you work(ed) for does

  • List education, system experience and volunteering at the end of the CV

  • There is no need to include references or to state that 'references can be provided on request' as that is a given
