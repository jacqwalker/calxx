Lessons from Roger Federer’s Fairy Tale 2017

20 December 2017

As 2017 draws to a close, it’s common to look back on the year’s successes and failures. Henry Ford said, “Failure is the opportunity to begin again, this time more intelligently”. So, let’s not focus on the failures, and instead take the time to revisit the lessons from 2017 and put them to use to make 2018 a successful year. To find the inspiration for the new year, lets draw on Roger Federer’s fairy tale 2017.

In January, Roger Federer secured his 18th Grand Slam, winning the Australian Open. At 35 years old, he is the oldest man in 45 years to win a Grand Slam. This was followed up by his record-breaking 8th Wimbledon Championship in July. Having withdrawn from the tour for six months in 2016 due to recurring back injuries, he looked set for retirement. Many pundits had written him off because of his injuries, his age, and the dominance of Novak Djokovic in the sport.

How did a former champion who hadn’t won a Grand Slam in nearly five years, turn it around? ‘If you believe, you can go really, really far in your life. I kept on believing and dreaming,’ Federer said in his post-match interview at Wimbledon. We can be quick to think that these talented athletes have it all, that they live a life that we can only dream about, a life where it all comes so easily to them. But, imagine not having had a major win in five years? For even the greatest athletes in the world, things don’t always go to plan. It’s their resilience and belief in themselves that we need to learn from.

The post-match interview gives us a glimpse of the inner workings of a tennis legend. He believed in himself. He kept working towards his goal. He did not listen to the critics or compare himself to others. Nor did he give up when he fell short. To sum it up: Federer knew what he wanted, he worked hard to get it and never wavered in his self-belief.

One of the biggest challenges we often face, is not knowing what we want. We drift through life, half-heartedly trying one thing after another with very little success. One of the best ways to figure out what you want in 2018 is to look forward to 31 December and ask yourself, what would have had to have happened to have made 2018 the best year yet? If you come up with a list, say 25 items, you can then use Warren Buffet’s tip to prioritise. Use your list to identify the five most important goals, separate them from the rest, and never look at the bottom twenty again. Those twenty items may be worthwhile, they may be things you would like to do, but they only serve to distract you from the five that really matter.

Let 2018 be your fairy tale year. Figure out what you want, work hard, don’t listen to the critics and don’t compare yourself to what everyone else is doing. And if you can only remember one thing from this article, let it be this: keep believing and dreaming because anything is possible.
